Research Interests

Research Statement

My primary research interest lies in statistical modeling and data-driven inference, with a particular focus on time series analysis, regression modeling, and experimental design.

During my undergraduate studies at National Dong Hwa University, I developed a strong foundation in statistical theory and its practical applications. My academic projects include regression analysis, likelihood ratio test derivations, and time series modeling using ARIMA and GARCH models on financial data.

I am particularly interested in understanding how statistical methods can be applied to real-world problems, especially in financial data analysis and predictive modeling. My work on MediaTek stock data reflects my interest in modeling both mean behavior and volatility structure.

In graduate study, I aim to deepen my knowledge in statistical inference and computational methods, and to contribute to research that bridges theoretical development with applied data analysis.

Ultimately, I aspire to become a researcher who applies statistical tools to extract meaningful insights from complex data.
